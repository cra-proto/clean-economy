Your reporting responsibilities

There are additional reporting responsibilities if you claimed the Clean Electricity ITC for a qualified natural gas energy system.

On this page

  • Annual emission intensity reporting
  • Penalty for failure to file annual emission intensity report
  • Repaying credit amounts to the CRA

Annual emission intensity reporting

If you claimed the Clean Electricity ITC for a qualified natural gas energy system, you must:

  • File a compliance report with both the CRA and NRCan
  • File every year for the first 20 years the system is operating and within 180 days after the end of each of these first 20 operating years
  • File as required, including the information listed below:
    • The actual emission intensity of the electrical energy produced by the system during the year
    • The gigawatt hours of electrical energy produced during the operating year
    • Any shutdown time of the system in respect of the year
    • o For the fifth operating year, the compliance report must include a report prepared by a qualified verification firm that verifies the actual emission intensity of the system for each operating year in the compliance period
  • Any shutdown time of the project for the year
  • For the 5th operating year, the compliance report must include a report that verifies the actual carbon intensity of the electricity produced during each operating year of the compliance period, prepared by a qualified verification firm for the project
  • Any information required in guidelines published by NRCan

Penalty for failure to file annual emission intensity report

If you do not file an annual emission intensity compliance report within 180 days after the end of each of the first 20 operating years for a qualified natural gas energy system, you are liable to a penalty that is 4% of the amount of the Clean Electricity ITC, prorated to provide a daily amount in respect of the failure to file.

The penalty will not exceed the total of all Clean Electricity ITCs you deducted for the system.

For each qualified natural gas energy system, the penalty is calculated as:

  • 4% of the total of all amounts of the Clean Electricity ITC for the qualified natural gas energy system deducted in tax years that ended before the deadline to file the compliance report
  • Multiplied by the number of days during which the failure continues
  • Divided by 365

Repaying credit amounts to the CRA

You may have to repay credit amounts you have already received.

Recovery tax on qualified natural gas energy systems

At the end of the compliance period of a qualified natural gas energy system, if the average actual emission intensity of the electricity produced is greater than 68.5 tonnes of carbon dioxide per gigawatt hour of electrical energy, you will be subject to recovery tax in the tax year in which the compliance period ends.

The recovery tax payable is determined by a formula applied to each qualified natural gas energy system.

The formula is (A – B) × C, where:

  • Variable A is the total amount of the Clean Electricity ITCs, each of which is based on the specified percentage that was applied to the capital cost of qualified natural gas energy equipment that was part of a system when determining a Clean Electricity ITC
  • Variable B is the total of all amounts, each of which can reasonably be considered to be the portion of any amount previously paid by the taxpayer due to a recapture for the equipment

The recovery tax is zero if the difference between the average actual carbon intensity and the expected carbon intensity of the project is 0.5 or less. This exception is intended to provide some tolerance and flexibility for variations in a project’s carbon intensity that are unanticipated or outside of your control.

NRCan will review each of the compliance reports and the CRA, in consultation with NRCan, may make a determination or redetermination of the actual carbon intensity of the electricity produced in the clean electricity project for any operating year during the compliance period of the project.

Interest on recovery tax

For the purpose of applying interest to an amount payable because of a recovery tax, your balance-due day for the tax year is deemed to be the balance-due day of the tax year for the related Clean Electricity ITC. This may create a liability for interest from the tax year in which the tax credit was originally claimed.

Recapture of Clean Electricity ITC

If you acquired an eligible clean electricity property in the year or any of the preceding 20 calendar years, and you became entitled to a Clean Electricity ITC for the capital cost or a portion of the capital cost, of the property, you will have to calculate a recapture of Clean Electricity ITC if in the tax year you:

  • Disposed of the property
  • Converted the property to a non-electricity or ammonia use
  • Exported the property from Canada

Recapture does not apply on the disposition of a property if the property was previously converted to a non-electricity or ammonia use or exported from Canada, which ensures that recapture is not triggered twice for the same property.

Where recapture applies in respect of an eligible clean electricity property, you must add to the tax otherwise payable for the year the amount determined by the formula (A – B) × (C ÷ D), where:

  • Variable A is the amount of your clean electricity tax credit in respect of the property
  • Variable B is the portion of any recovery tax you previously paid in respect of the property
  • Variable C is the amount, not exceeding the original capital cost of the property, equal to either the proceeds of disposition of the property or the fair market value of the property, depending on the circumstances
  • Variable D is the capital cost of the property on which the Clean Electricity ITC was deducted

Election – sale of clean electricity project

You may make an election to avoid recapture in certain circumstances.

The election may be available where a qualifying taxpayer (the vendor) disposes of all or substantially all of its properties that are part of a qualified clean electricity project of the taxpayer to another taxable Canadian corporation (the purchaser).

The vendor and the purchaser must jointly elect in prescribed form on or before the day that is earliest of the day the vendor or the purchaser is required to file a return for the tax year in which the transaction occurred. If this election is made, the purchaser will assume the relevant tax history of the vendor so that recovery and recapture taxes under section 127.48 can apply appropriately.

Actual emission intensity

Actual emission intensity means the emission intensity of a specified natural gas energy system of a qualifying entity, based on the actual carbon dioxide emissions from the production of electrical energy by the system.

Qualified verification firm

A qualified verification firm is a person or company that is allowed to verify a taxpayer’s natural gas energy system.

To qualify, they must meet all of the following conditions:

  • They must be either:
    • A licensed engineer, or
    • An engineering firm that is officially registered and in good standing with a recognized engineering association
  • That association must be legally authorized to regulate engineers in:
    • The province or territory where the energy system is located, or
    • If no such association exists there, any other Canadian jurisdiction that does regulate engineers
  • They must have appropriate insurance coverage to protect against risks related to their work
  • They must be completely independent from the taxpayer:
    • Not employed by the taxpayer or the qualified validation firm
    • oMust deal with the taxpayer or the qualified validation firm at arm’s length (for example , no close business or personal relationship)
  • They must meet the standards outlined in guidelines published by the Minister of Natural Resources
  • They must have specialized knowledge in auditing systems that monitor carbon dioxide emissions
  • Specifically, they need to understand how to verify compliance with the Regulations Limiting Carbon Dioxide Emissions from Natural Gas-fired Generation of Electricity

Operating year

Operating year of a specified natural gas energy system, means each cumulative 365-day period, the first of which begins on the start-up date of a qualifying entity's specified natural gas energy system, disregarding any period during which the system is not operating.

Compliance period

Compliance period in respect of a specified natural gas energy system of a taxpayer, means the period beginning on the start-up date of the system and ending on the last day of the fifth operating year of the system.
